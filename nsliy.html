006 : NSLI-Y


June 2018 - Aug 2018
Northwest University
Xi'an, Shaanxi, China

NSLI-Y stands for National Security Language Initiative for Youth. It is merit-based scholarship funded by the U.S. Department of State to study critical languages abroad. For the summer semester I lived with a host family in Xi'an. I studied at Northwest University and attended 4 hours of language classes in the morning and 4 hours of activity in the afternoon that consisted of cultural classes, volunteering, and language study.

At the end of the program we took a HSK (Hanyu Shuiping Kaoshi) exam to evaluate our language success. I achieved HSK Level 2 in speaking and comprehension (writing was not tested). Throughout my university career, I have continued to learn Mandarin with the coursework in RPI's foreign language department.
