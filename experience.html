Undergradute Research

August 2020 - May 2021

Troy, NY

During my sophomore year, I worked with Dr. Nidhi Rastogi and Dr. Alex Gittens on a series of Natural Language Processing and Optimizaion projects that focued on malware threat analysis and truth detection.


Going into this reasearch opportunity, I did not have much experience with natural language processing, but I was familiar with malware characteristics, so we spent the first part of the research time reviewing papers on entity extraction and relationship prediction. Then we worked on developping our own entity and relationship extraction technique that was specifically for threat reports. This was done in Python, primarily using the Pytorch, Numpy, Matplotlib, and Scikit-Learn libraries. The knowledge graph part of this project was done with Neo4j, however I focues primarily on the entity and relationship extraction part. You can find our paper on the Cornell E-print Repository.


The second major area focused on estimating trustworthiness. This was done primarily with optimiztion algorithms.
